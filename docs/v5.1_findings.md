# HyperBot V5.1 — Backtest Debugging & Findings

## 1. The "0 Trades" Investigation
During the initial backtesting of V5.1 using `sweeper.js`, the strategy consistently yielded exactly 0 trades across all multipliers. The investigation led to two distinct categories of issues: execution bugs in the sweeper itself, and a core mismatch in volatility logic.

### Missing Data & Sweeper Bugs
1. **The Null ATR Bug:** `multi_sim.js` utilizes `indicators.fastAtr` as a strict gatekeeper before allowing any entries. Because `sweeper.js` bypassed the live boot sequence, the ATR indicator remained `null`, causing the engine to instantly reject every potential setup.
   - **Fix:** We exported the `indicators` object from `multi_sim.js` and implemented a live 14-period True Range rolling average directly inside the `sweeper.js` tick loop.
2. **Incorrect Volume Profiling Key:** The sweeper was injecting ticks using `{ sz: tradeSize }`, but `multi_sim.js` expected `{ size: tradeSize }` for its volume and CVD calculations, inadvertently causing the bot to calculate 0 volume.
   - **Fix:** Standardized the simulated trade object to match the live engine expectations (`price`, `size`, `side`, `time`).

## 2. Advanced Backtest Synthesis (backtest_fix.txt)
To make the sweeper a realistic testing environment that could actually trigger the advanced CVD parameters, we implemented the requested physics:
- **Directional Volume Bias:** Instead of 50/50 randomized tick sides, the sweeper evaluates if the 1-minute historical candle closed green or red. It then skews the synthetic ticks **80/20** in that direction to accurately simulate momentum order flow.
- **Dynamic VWAP Spikes:** `vwapState` was imported directly from `feed.js` and is updated recursively on every tick. If a synthetic tick lands within the `0.2%` VWAP proximity zone, the `tradeSize` is dynamically amplified by **3x** to mirror the real-world front-running liquidity we are trying to capture.

## 3. The Root Cause: Volatility Mismatch
Even after implementing the backtest physics and fixing the missing indicator bugs, the bot *still* yielded 0 trades. The logs revealed why:
```text
[Consider] Touched VWAP, but volatility is too low. ATR $39.00 <= Hurdle $45.89. Stand down.
```

**The Volatility Hurdle Paradox:**
The strategy requires the current ATR to be strictly greater than the `atrHurdle` (`midPrice * volMultiplier`).
- At a BTC price of $65,000, your lowest V5.1 multiplier of `0.0007` created a hurdle of **$45.50**.
- The historical dataset provided (`historical_candles_1m.json`) represents an exceptionally low-volatility period, with the 1-minute candle ATR lingering between **$25.00 - $40.00**.
- Because the market was physically incapable of producing enough volatility to satisfy the baseline multiplier, the bot correctly identified the setups as "too slow/chop" and aborted every entry.

### Resolution
To force the bot to trade in this specific historical slice without artificially crippling the live logic, we expanded the test matrix in `sweeper.js` to include much lower multipliers (`0.0004`, `0.0005`, `0.0006`). This maps correctly to the historical $25-$40 ATR range. 

**Takeaway:** If the bot goes silent during a live weekend or off-peak hours, the `volMultiplier` floor needs to dynamically scale down, or we must accept that the bot will refuse to play in the mud.
